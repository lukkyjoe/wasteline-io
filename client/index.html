The Problem

Cities spend hundreds of thousands of dollars per year to teach people how to recycle. But recycling rates across the US have stagnated the last 5 years; meanwhile, contamination levels in the recycle supply have risen because people care enough to try to recycle, but arenâ€™t informed enough to recycle correctly. To make matters worse, cities have different policies on recycling. For example, did you know that your Whole Foods salads bags is accepted by the city of San Francisco for recycling but not by the city of Berkeley?

Learn More

Our Solution

The way information is currently served is broken, confusing, and too costly. Wasteline's solution is this to build the knowledge base on the disposability of every single product; one that lets you filter by the brand or manufacturer, and look up the ecological impact of that product's life cycle. And we want to harness community wisdom to build that knowledge base. Think of it as a wikipedia for waste.

We're serving three kinds of users

Our solution will tighten the feedback loop between consumers, government agencies, and brands.
